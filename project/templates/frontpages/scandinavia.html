Day 2 - Arrival in Copenhagen

Copenhagen, Denmark’s capital, sits on the coastal islands of Zealand and Amager, linked to Malmo in southern Sweden by the Öresund Bridge. Indre By, the central district, contains 18th-century, rococo Frederiksstaden, home to the royal family’s Amalienborg Palace. The city's center also has the Christiansborg parliament building and the Renaissance Rosenborg Castle, which has a museum of royal artifacts and a popular garden.

After picking you up from the Airport, we'll head into town and check into our extremely central accommodation.




(Optional day trips)

Tivoli Park - Tivoli Gardens is a famous amusement park and pleasure garden in Copenhagen, Denmark. The park opened on 15 August 1843 and is the second-oldest operating amusement park in the world, after Dyrehavsbakken in nearby Klampenborg, also in Denmark.




Day 6 - Ferry to Stavanger

Get a chance to truly ride like a local. The ferry has onboard bars, restaurants, music, dancing, and even a casino!

Not to mention a seriously cool experience with some great scenery.

Day 7 - Arrive in Stavanger

Stavanger is a city and municipality in Norway. The city is the third-largest urban zone and metropolitan area in Norway and the administrative centre of Rogaland county. The municipality is the fourth most populous in Norway

Arriving in Stavanger, We'll check into our hotel and take a brief walk around the city. The nightlife in Stavanger is well known, so we'll have plently of options for tonight!




Days 10 and 11 - Bergen

Bergen is a city on Norway’s southwestern coast surrounded by mountains and fjords, including Sognefjord, the country’s longest and deepest. The Bryggen district consists of the old wharf and its surrounding commercial buildings (a former center of the league of Hanseatic merchants), and features vibrantly colored wooden houses.

We'll also get to see Mt Fløyen. One of the most iconic attractions of Norway is the funicular Floibanen in Bergen, which takes you to a stunning view-point at Mt Fløyen. Where we'll have incredible views of Bergen and the surrounding areas.




Day 13 - Train to Oslo

Modern air-conditioned trains link Oslo with Bergen, one of Europe's most scenic train routes, a journey worth doing for its own sake!

Day 14 and 15 - OSLO

Some of us will leave (The 14 day people), the rest will continue on!
Oslo, the capital of Norway, sits on the country’s southern coast at the head of the Oslofjord, and is known for its citywide green spaces and museums. Many of these are on the Bygdoy peninsula, including the Norwegian Maritime Museum, the Vikingskipshuset, with ships from the 10th century, and the Norsk Folkemuseum, with artifacts from Sami and Viking cultures.

Oslo offers a TON of optional day trips and things to see.

Day 18 - Flight home

After saying our goodbyes to Africa, we head to the airport for your flight back home.

Day 1 - Overnight Flight to Copenhagen

Overnight flight and transfer to Copenhagen. The total flight is around 8-10 hours, plus customs & other assorted inconveniences.
















Days 3 and 4 - Free Days in Copenhagen

Copenhagen has ALOT to see. From the 'liberal friendly' Christiania to Tivoli park. It's impossible to be bored here.




(Optional day trips)

Day trip to Malmö -
Malmö is the capital and largest city of the Swedish county of Scania. Malmö is the third largest city in Sweden, after Stockholm and Gothenburg, and the fifth largest city in Scandinavia, with a population of above 300,000.




Day 5 - TRAIN TO AALBORG

We start our long journey outside of Copenhagen. The first stop Aalborg.
Aalborg is a city in the Jutland region of Denmark. It’s known for its revitalized waterfront on the Limfjord, the body of water that cuts through Jutland. Also notable are the Aalborg Havnebad outdoor pool, exhibitions at the Utzon Center and concerts at the futuristic House of Music. Nearby is the 16th-century, half-timbered Aalborghus Castle. The Aalborg Historical Museum tells the city’s 1,000-year history.






Day 8 - Trip to Preikestolen

Preikestolen or Prekestolen, also known by the English translations of Preacher's Pulpit or Pulpit Rock, is a famous tourist attraction in Forsand, Ryfylke, Norway.

We'll take a short day trip to Preikestolen (or Pulpit Rock). One of the most iconic sights in Norway. Once there, it's a two hour moderate hike to the top.




Day 9 - Last day in Stavanger

Some of our group will be leaving today (The 9 day people). The rest can take a day trip or just relax from the hike.

Some day trips include Kjerag, a Fjord boat tours, and much more...! At night, the party is always going!




Day 12 - Train to Flam

Flåm is a village in southwestern Norway, in an area known for its fjords. It sits at the end of Aurlandsfjord, a branch of the vast Sognefjord. The dramatic Stegastein viewing platform juts out high above the Aurlandsfjord. South of Flåm Harbor, the 17th-century wooden Flåm Church lies in the valley. The Flåm Railway offers valley and waterfall views as it climbs to a station on the Hardangervidda plateau.

Called one of the most scenic train rides in the world.




Day 16 and 17 - Stockholm

Our final stop on the trip!

Stockholm, the capital of Sweden, encompasses 14 islands and more than 50 bridges on an extensive Baltic Sea archipelago. The cobblestone streets and ochre-colored buildings of Gamla Stan (the old town) are home to the 13th-century Storkyrkan Cathedral, the Kungliga Slottet Royal Palace and the Nobel Museum, which focuses on the Nobel Prize. Ferries and sightseeing boats shuttle passengers between the islands.

Safety in Africa:
Tanzania is one of the safest countries in Africa.
Crime is usually only theft, at it's worst (which is even rare), people are incredibly friendly and welcoming; still using common sense goes a long way. You should NOT be out walking alone at night or in general.
Kilimanjaro is an extremely safe climb; with huts at most stops and popular routes, problems (if they occur) are addressed immediately. Still Every year people get hurt or die. Usually from poor planning or from using a poor guide service. A mountian climb is NOT the time to be cheap or "just wing it".
During the safari, most animals will either completely ignore you or try to steal your food. Unless provoked or threatened. Please do not antagonize the animals.
Nairobi CAN BE a little dangerous, but is an extremely interesting city if you travel smart.

The lesson is to exercise travel smarts and common sense. LISTEN TO YOUR GUIDES! They are here to make sure you're having the best possible experience.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the tipping policy?
Tipping for the Kilimanjaro climb averages around $300/per person. This includes all of the porters, cooks, and guides. Upwards of 20-30 per group.
While our local guides are paid much higher than other companies and tipping is not 'required', it is highly recommended; Every dollar means a ton in the world of tips.

What should I pack for the climb and for Africa?
Please check our packing page dedicated to Africa.

What kind of physical condition should I be in?
This question is a bit harder to answer. People in incredible physical shape can get altitude sickness just as bad as people in horrible shape.
But as a general guideline; the climb is very difficult. Do not underestimate it. You should be in excellent ccardiovascular shape. DO NOT attempt if you have pre-existing heart conditions or breathing conditions (Unless your doctor approves it).

Do I need any vaccine's?
We suggest going to the CDC's website. But a Yellow fever vaccine is a must have.
